Privacy Policy

Last updated: 28 May 2026

Recalla is built on a simple principle: your data belongs to you. Everything you store in Recalla stays on your device. We never collect it, store it, or see it.

1. What data Recalla stores

Recalla stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Names, roles, and contact details you enter for people
  • Notes, memories, and tags you create
  • Voice recordings you make within the app, and their transcriptions
  • Dates, reminders, and any other information you add
  • Your personal card details (name, role, contact info) if you choose to create one

All of this data is stored exclusively on your device using local storage. It is never transmitted to any server, cloud service, or third party — except as described in Section 3 below for optional AI features.

2. What data we collect

We collect no personal data. Recalla does not require an account, login, or any form of registration.

We do not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any identifying information
  • The content of your notes or memories
  • Your location
  • Usage analytics or behavioural data
  • Crash reports tied to your identity

3. Third-party services

Recalla integrates with the following third-party services in specific, limited circumstances:

  • Google Gemini API — used only when you explicitly activate AI features (Voice Memory or Quick Brief). When you use these features, the text of your memory notes is sent to Google's Gemini API for processing. Your contact names, personal details, and all other data are never sent. AI features are entirely optional; the core app works fully offline without them. Google's use of this data is governed by Google's privacy policy.
  • RevenueCat — used to process in-app purchases (Recalla Pro and Recalla AI subscriptions). RevenueCat receives a pseudonymous device identifier and purchase transaction data to verify your subscription status. No personal data or app content is shared. See RevenueCat's privacy policy.
  • Google Play Store — the app is distributed via Google Play. Google may collect certain technical information as part of the standard app distribution process, subject to Google's privacy policy.

4. Permissions

Recalla may request the following device permissions:

  • Contacts — used only for one-tap import of people from your address book into Recalla. Recalla never syncs data back to your contacts or modifies your address book.
  • Microphone — used only when you choose to record a voice memory. Audio is processed locally and, if you use the AI transcription feature, the resulting text is sent to Google Gemini. The raw audio never leaves your device.
  • Camera — used only to scan a QR code from another user's Recalla card. No photos are taken or stored.
  • Notifications — used only to deliver reminders you have set yourself, such as birthday alerts or memory reminders.

No permission is requested without a clear in-app reason, and you may revoke any permission at any time via your device settings.

5. Your digital card

Recalla lets you create a personal digital card with your name, role, and contact details. This card is stored only on your device. It is shared exclusively when you take a deliberate action — tapping "Share" or showing your QR code. Nothing from your card is uploaded automatically or accessible to anyone without your explicit action.

6. Data export and deletion

You own your data completely. You can export all your data at any time from within the app. Deleting the app from your device permanently removes all data Recalla has stored — there is no backup on our side to delete.

7. Children's privacy

Recalla is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

8. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will update the date at the top of this page and note the change in the app's release notes. Your continued use of the app after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
